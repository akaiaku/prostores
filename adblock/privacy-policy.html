✅ One-line Summary

AdBlock: Privacy & Focus does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. All settings are stored locally on your device. We never see your browsing history.

This extension is a client-side ad and tracker blocker. Its sole purpose is to block ads, trackers, and malicious domains using Chrome's built-in declarativeNetRequest API, and to offer optional Focus Mode and referrer anonymization tools.

📦 Data We Collect

Data stored locally on your device (never transmitted):

Data Purpose Where stored
Blocking on/off state Remember your preference across sessions chrome.storage.local
Paused / allowlisted domains Skip blocking on sites you choose chrome.storage.local
Focus Mode domains & timer Block distracting sites during focus sessions chrome.storage.local
Privacy settings (referrer toggle) Apply your chosen privacy settings chrome.storage.local
Blocking stats (counts) Show you how many requests were blocked chrome.storage.local
Cached remote blocklist rules Apply up-to-date malware blocking without re-fetching every page load chrome.storage.local

Data we do NOT collect: browsing history, URLs you visit, search queries, form inputs, passwords, IP addresses, device identifiers, or any personally identifiable information.

🔑 Permissions Explained

The extension requests the minimum permissions required to function:

declarativeNetRequest storage alarms <all_urls>
Permission Why it is needed
declarativeNetRequest Blocks ad, tracker, and malware network requests at the browser level. Also used for referrer header anonymization (modifyHeaders). Chrome enforces all rules internally — the extension never reads request content.
storage Saves your settings, allowlist, and stats locally via chrome.storage.local. Nothing is synced to any server.
alarms Schedules a daily background task to refresh remote malware blocklists, and to auto-expire Focus Mode timers even when the extension popup is closed.
<all_urls> (host permission) Required by declarativeNetRequest to apply blocking rules across all websites. Without broad host access, network-level blocking cannot work on arbitrary URLs. The extension does not read page content via this permission.

🌐 Remote Data Fetching

The extension periodically fetches plain-text domain blocklists from two trusted public sources:

  • URLhaus (abuse.ch) — urlhaus.abuse.ch/downloads/hostfile/
    A community-maintained feed of live malware distribution domains.
  • Phishing Army — phishing.army/download/phishing_army_blocklist.txt
    An aggregated blocklist of known phishing domains.
These requests fetch data only — plain text files containing domain names, one per line. No JavaScript is downloaded or executed from remote servers. The fetched domains are converted locally into declarativeNetRequest rules.

These fetch requests are made by the extension's service worker, not on behalf of any specific web page you visit. They do not carry your browsing history or identity. Updates occur at most once every 24 hours.

🔗 Third-Party Services

The extension does not integrate any third-party analytics, crash reporting, advertising, or tracking SDKs. The only external network requests are the blocklist fetches described in Section 4 above.

The extension's source code does not include any monetization scripts, sponsored whitelists, or "acceptable ads" programs. All blocking rules are applied equally.

🚫 Data Sharing & Sale

We do not sell, share, rent, or trade any user data with any third party, ever.

Because no personal data is collected, there is nothing to share. All processing happens entirely within your browser using local storage and Chrome's native APIs.

👶 Children's Privacy

This extension does not collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. It complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) by design — no data collection takes place.
